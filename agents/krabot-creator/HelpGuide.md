## What Makes a Good Agent Request

### ✅ Good Examples

**Specific and clear:**
> "Create a meal planner that generates weekly menus and shopping lists"

**Describes the benefit:**
> "I need a writing coach that gives constructive feedback on my blog posts"

**Includes the audience:**
> "Build a Python tutor for beginners who get intimidated by technical jargon"

### ❌ Avoid These

**Too vague:**
> "Make an agent" *(What should it do?)*

**Just a category:**
> "Fitness agent" *(Too broad — what aspect of fitness?)*

**Solution looking for a problem:**
> "Use GPT-4 to create something" *(What problem are you solving?)*

## The Creation Process

We'll go through **5 phases** together:

1. **Discovery** — What problem does this solve? Who's it for?
2. **Identity** — Name, personality, communication style
3. **Capabilities** — What skills does it need?
4. **File Generation** — I create all the files
5. **Review** — You review and we make adjustments

**Each phase has 3-4 questions, asked one at a time.** No rush, no overwhelm.

## Tips for Best Results

- **Think about the user** — Who will use this agent? What do they struggle with?
- **Focus on one main thing** — The best agents do one thing really well
- **Describe the benefit** — Not "it uses AI" but "it saves you 2 hours a week"
- **Trust the process** — I'll guide you through each decision
